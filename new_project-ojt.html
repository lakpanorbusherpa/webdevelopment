NETWORKING

Networking is a crucial subject in the Bachelor of Computer

Applications (BCA) curriculum. It encompasses the study of

how computers and other devices communicate with each other,

sharing resources and information. Networking is the backbone

of modern information technology and plays a significant role

in various applications, from simple file sharing to complex

cloud computing environments.

Students in BCA learn about different types of networks,

including Local Area Networks (LAN), Wide Area Networks

(WAN), and Metropolitan Area Networks (MAN). They study

network topologies, such as star, ring, and mesh, which

define the layout of network connections. The course also

covers networking models, like the OSI (Open Systems

Interconnection) model and the TCP/IP model, which

standardize communication protocols.

Understanding IP addressing is fundamental,

as it allows devices to be identified on a network.

Students learn about IPv4 and IPv6, subnetting, and

the role of DNS (Domain Name System) in resolving

human-readable addresses to IP addresses. Networking also

involves studying various communication protocols, such as

HTTP, FTP, SMTP, and more, which facilitate data exchange over

the internet.

Another important aspect of networking in BCA is network

security. Students explore different types of threats and

vulnerabilities, as well as measures to protect networks,

including firewalls, encryption, and intrusion detection systems.
